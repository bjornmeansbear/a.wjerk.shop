Five case studies that form a coherent through-line: design as systems thinking, material responsibility, and open/commons-based practice — developed consistently over 15+ years. The goal is a new portfolio site (dot work dot shop) that leads with these projects rather than traditional graphic design work.

Case studies — in narrative order
Sustainabilitist Principles — MFA Thesis, ~2009
The foundational piece. A research synthesis distilled into aphorisms, printed as book plates, physically connected to source books with embroidery floss that trailed onto the floor — showing ideas flowing in from sources and back out into the world. The poster/installation was designed to be temporary: it came together for the exhibition, then returned to its constituent parts. Origin point for everything that followed.

Green Acres → EcoVention Europe — Two books, one evolution
Treat as a single case study showing thinking evolve. Green Acres: tried to be "less bad" — better paper, print-on-demand, smaller batches. EcoVention Europe: pushed further — minimized negative space, used ink mixes with lower color percentages, eliminated bleeds, reused the typeface and grid. More content, barely more pages. A demonstration of constraint-based design thinking applied to publishing.

MICA Grad Admissions Materials — Open source, education-as-design
Admissions materials where imagery and typefaces were sourced entirely from public domain and open source. The piece tells you it did this and shows you where to find those resources yourself — so receiving the mailer is already an education in open design. Also minimized printing and mailing footprint. Shows applying these principles inside an institution, in client work.

Spontaneous Lamps — Circular making
Personal project. When you need a lamp, instead of buying one, construct it from lamp-like things already at hand. Sometimes one-use, sometimes permanent — but designed to dissolve back into raw materials when no longer needed. A tangible demonstration of circular thinking, material reuse, and design-as-problem-solving rather than consumption. Needs better photo documentation.

People Processing Plastic — Community engagement + circular economy pedagogy
Started as a personal project (building a Precious Plastics-style workshop), grew into a collaborative project with colleagues, then into classroom curriculum in the circular economy design course. Array of plastic grinding/melting machines used in public workshops — but the goal isn't making stuff, it's using the performance of recycling as a way to have real conversations with regular people about plastic and material systems. Exhibitions, grants, ongoing.

Honorable mention — speculative / conceptual
Carbon-Sequestering Book — Speculative project
Hypothetical: a book printed with ink that absorbs carbon from the air, visibly darkening over time as it pulls carbon down. Never built, but discussed with real people — and the conversations it sparked were part of what led to Climate Designers forming. Worth including as a speculative probe. Sits between the books and the making projects in the narrative arc.
